### Re-analysis of Turbulence Robustness Data

The experimental datasets in this repository are from the study:

Guo, Z., Peters, C., Mata-Cervera, N., Vetlugin, A., Guo, R., Zhang, P., ... & Shen, Y. (2026). "Topological robustness of classical and quantum optical skyrmions in atmospheric turbulence." *Nature Communications*. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-026-68751-3

The corresponding replication data is available at:  
Mata-Cervera, N., Guo, Z., & Shen, Y. (2026). Replication Data for: Topological robustness of classical and quantum optical skyrmions in atmospheric turbulence. DR-NTU (Data). https://doi.org/10.21979/N9/8LMJ3I

In the original work, coincidence counts were recorded for entangled photon pairs (polarization + OAM) under varying simulated atmospheric turbulence strengths. The key result was that topological invariants (e.g., skyrmion numbers) remain highly stable even as raw entanglement (concurrence/purity) degrades significantly with turbulence.

We re-analyzed the same raw coincidence tables through the 3D Information Theory framework by normalizing counts into weights (sum-to-1), applying noise proportional to turbulence strength, and computing entropy, entanglement strength E, and the derived level-19 constant-gradient plateau under Uniform, Exponential, and especially Fibonacci weightings. The analysis shows that the level-19 plateau and protected \( c_{\text{eff}} \approx 0.38 \) exhibit greater stability than raw entanglement metrics across the tested turbulence range, with Fibonacci weighting enhancing resilience at higher depths. This offers a recursive informational mechanism for the observed topological robustness.

Full re-analysis code (`turbulence_reanalysis.py`) and results are included in the repository.
